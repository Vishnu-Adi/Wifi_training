# Assignment 1: In which OSI layer does Wi‑Fi fit?

Wi‑Fi is not limited to just one OSI layer. It mainly works in **Layer 1 (Physical Layer)** and **Layer 2 (Data Link Layer)**.

At the **Physical Layer**, Wi‑Fi is responsible for sending and receiving radio signals through the air. This includes things like frequency bands, modulation, channel width, and how bits are turned into wireless signals.

At the **Data Link Layer**, Wi‑Fi handles how devices share the wireless medium, how frames are formed, and how devices communicate using MAC addresses. This is where the **802.11 MAC protocol** works, along with functions like authentication, association, retransmission control, and medium access.

So, in simple viva language:

**Wi‑Fi belongs to Layer 1 and Layer 2 of the OSI model.**

- **Layer 1:** wireless transmission of signals
- **Layer 2:** framing, MAC addressing, and access control

If you want to be a little more precise, you can say:

> Wi‑Fi is an IEEE 802.11 technology that defines both the Physical layer and the MAC sublayer of the Data Link layer.

That is the best short answer for an assignment or viva.
